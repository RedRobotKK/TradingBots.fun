## What Could Go Wrong (And What to Expect)

### Scenario 1: Market is Too Choppy (ADX < 20)

**What happens:**
- Trend-following strategies return Err
- Mean reversion triggers more often (but shorter lives)
- Win rate might drop to 55-65%

**Expected return:** +5% to +12% (vs +12% to +20% normal)

**What to do:**
- This is NORMAL in consolidation markets
- Reduce position size to 3-5% (vs 8-12%)
- Focus on shorter-duration trades (1-4 hours)

### Scenario 2: High Volatility (ATR > 5)

**What happens:**
- Bollinger Bands widen significantly
- Stops get hit more often
- Need tighter confluence (7-9 signals required)

**Expected return:** +8% to +15% (slightly lower)

**What to do:**
- Require 8+ signals before trading (vs 7)
- Use wider stops (4% vs 3%)
- Reduce leverage to 8x max

### Scenario 3: Strong Directional Bias (One-sided market)

**What happens:**
- All indicators point same direction
- Fewer opportunities on the other side
- Fewer total trades but higher accuracy

**Expected return:** +15% to +25% (can be HIGHER)

**What to do:**
- Let it run! High confluence trades are the best
- Might see 80%+ win rate
- Larger position sizes are OK

### Scenario 4: One Strategy Dominates Badly

**Example:** Stochastic signals every hour and loses 40% of the time

**What to do:**
- Check if it's a solo signal (bad) or in confluence (OK)
- If solo: Disable it or increase confidence requirement
- If in confluence: It's adding noise, reduce weight
- Remove if it causes >30% of losses
